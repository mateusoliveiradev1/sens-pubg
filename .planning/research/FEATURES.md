# Research: Feature And Monetization Findings

**Date:** 2026-05-05

## Paid Value Patterns In Aim Products

Competitors and adjacent products sell one or more of these:

- Advanced metrics and trend insight.
- Sensitivity finder or calibration.
- Guided training programs and daily missions.
- AI coach or expert guidance.
- Adaptive tasks or personalized routines.
- Benchmarks, leaderboards, progression, and community.
- Courses, academy content, creator/pro guidance.

Sens PUBG has a different asset: it can analyze the player's actual PUBG spray clip instead of only training in a simulator. That should be the central paid value.

## Recommended Free/Paid Split

### Free

- Limited analyses per week/month.
- Basic clip quality report.
- Basic diagnosis and one coach summary.
- Public community viewing.
- Clear "confidence" and "not enough evidence" states.

### Pro Monthly

- More analyses.
- Full coach plan and next-block protocol.
- Multi-clip trend and history.
- Advanced metrics: confidence, coverage, phase, residual, distance-adjusted error.
- Compare current clip vs previous blocks.
- Export/share premium report.
- Access to structured spray mastery programs.

### Team/Coach Expansion

- Coach dashboard for multiple players.
- Squad/team reports.
- Shared review queue.
- Player progression snapshots.
- Seat-based/team subscription.

### Credits Later

- Good for one-off premium review, LLM-heavy reports, or specialist/manual review.
- Do not start here unless subscription conversion is weak.

## Product-Led Growth Notes

Freemium needs a fast activation moment. For Sens PUBG, activation should be:

"The user uploads one usable clip and sees one useful, confidence-scored next-block protocol."

Upgrade prompts should trigger after value, not before value:

- After a successful analysis.
- After the free user wants to compare multiple clips.
- After the user hits a recurring training loop.
- When team/coach behavior appears, such as sharing reports or reviewing multiple players.
