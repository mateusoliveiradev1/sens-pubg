# Research: Market And Stack Signals

**Date:** 2026-05-05

## Local Product Stack

Sens PUBG already has the technical base needed for a premium spray improvement product:

- Next.js App Router, React, TypeScript, Drizzle, Postgres, Auth.js, Vercel.
- Browser-first video analysis through Web Workers.
- Deterministic tracking/diagnostics/sensitivity/coach pipeline.
- Optional Groq/OpenAI-compatible LLM rewrite that cannot change technical facts.
- Golden, benchmark, captured clips, release, and readiness scripts.
- Community and entitlement scaffolding already present.

## External Market Signals

- PUBG remains a large active PC title. Steam's public stats page showed PUBG: BATTLEGROUNDS among the top Steam games on 2026-05-04, with 107,109 current players and 728,071 peak that day.
- KRAFTON has publicly framed PUBG as an expanding IP/content platform, including PUBG 2.0, UGC, Unreal Engine 5 direction, and broader franchise investment.
- Aimlabs+ monetizes advanced sensitivity tools, training programs, AI aim coach, adaptive tasks, academy content, community, and advanced metrics at $10/month monthly, $9/month quarterly, and $7/month six-month cadence.
- KovaaK's monetizes as a paid trainer on Steam with community-created scenarios, stats, leaderboards, workouts, competitions, and DLC.

## Implication

Sens PUBG should not try to be a generic aim trainer. The best wedge is PUBG-specific spray improvement from real clips:

1. Upload clip.
2. Get confidence-scored analysis.
3. Receive one next-block protocol.
4. Repeat clips over time.
5. See whether control, consistency, and confidence improved.
