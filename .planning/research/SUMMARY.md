# Research Summary: Spray Perfection And Monetization

**Date:** 2026-05-05

## Recommendation

Build Sens PUBG as a freemium monthly product around PUBG-specific spray improvement from real clips.

The strongest promise is:

"Upload a PUBG spray clip, get an honest analysis, and follow one testable coach protocol for the next block."

## Why This Direction

- PUBG still has a large active PC audience and KRAFTON continues to invest in PUBG as a broader franchise/content platform.
- Existing aim products prove that players pay for advanced metrics, sensitivity tools, guided programs, AI coaching, adaptive training, and progression.
- Sens PUBG's differentiation is not generic aim practice. It is real-clip spray analysis with PUBG-specific context and coach decisions.
- Freemium monthly fits the habit loop: analyze, train, repeat, compare, improve.

## Business Model

Start with:

- Free: limited analyses and basic coach summary.
- Pro: full coach plan, more analyses, trends/history, advanced metrics, repeat-clip comparison, premium training programs.
- Later: team/coach plan and credits for one-off premium/manual/LLM-heavy review.

Suggested first price test:

- Pro BR: R$19.90 to R$29.90/month.
- Pro global: roughly US$7 to US$10/month, matching the lower end of adjacent aim subscription pricing.
- Team/coach later: seat-based or small-team plan after solo retention is proven.

Do not lock this price as final until we measure activation and willingness to pay.

## Product Metrics To Track

- Activation: first usable clip analyzed.
- Aha moment: user sees a confidence-scored next-block protocol.
- Repeat value: second/third clip uploaded in 7 days.
- Coach outcome: protocol accepted, completed, improved, failed, or inconclusive.
- Upgrade signal: user wants more analyses, history comparison, advanced metrics, export/share, or program continuation.
- Retention: weekly repeat analysis/training loop.
- Revenue: free-to-paid conversion, paid churn, ARPU, and cost per free user.
